Across existing assets, three different name treatments are in play:

| Treatment | Where it appears |

**This MUST be resolved before launching GBP, Facebook, or any new asset.** Local SEO depends on Name/Address/Phone (NAP) being identical across every public reference. Google penalizes name drift.

**Recommendation:** Use **"Sir Box a Lot Movers"** (four separate words, no hyphens) as the brand display name everywhere except the LLC's legal filing. Reasons:

- Matches the logo and physical signage — the most-recognized treatment
- Cleanest read at a glance
- Avoids the hyphen ambiguity ("Box-a-Lot" vs "Box a Lot" in search queries)

## Positioning

**One-line:** Sir Box a Lot Movers is the friendly, hardworking labor-only moving crew for the Puget Sound — the people you call when you've got a U-Haul reserved and need a crew that'll actually hustle.

**Who we serve:** Renters, downsizers, young families, and value-conscious homeowners across Pierce, King, and Kitsap counties who want professional movers without the full-service mover price tag.

**What makes us different:**

1. **Labor-only model** — you rent the truck (U-Haul, Penske, Budget), we bring the muscle. Lower total cost than full-service movers.
2. **The crew actually hustles** — no leaning on the truck, no stretching breaks on your dime.
3. **Transparent billing** — 2-hour minimum, 15-minute increments after. No fuel surcharges, no hidden fees.
4. **A knight mascot people remember** — in a category where every competitor's logo looks the same, we stand out.

**Hybrid brand strategy:**

- Visual identity carries the **personality** (knight mascot, orange signage, friendly tone)
- Copy carries the **trust** (specifics, transparent pricing, no fluff)
- Together: memorable AND reliable, not just one or the other

## Voice & tone

The brand voice is **direct, confident, working-class craftsman.** Like a foreman talking to you straight — friendly, practical, no marketing fluff. The knight mascot does the cute work; the copy stays grounded.

### Voice characteristics

| Trait | Sounds like | Doesn't sound like |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Direct | "Strong backs. Careful hands. We hustle." | "Leveraging premier relocation solutions." |
| Confident | "We hustle between loads." | "We try our best." |
| Warm | "Real people. Real moves." | "Greetings, valued customer." |
| Specific | "15-minute increments after the 2-hour minimum." | "Competitive pricing." |
| Earnest | "Treat your belongings like our own." | "Going viral with the best vibes." |
| Light knight flair | "The crew your move deserves." | "Hark! Forsooth! Knaves and varlets!" |

### Sentence cadence

- Short sentences. Compound only when needed.
- Lead with the verb when possible ("Show up on time" not "We make sure to show up on time").
- Specifics beat adjectives. ("$125/hr" beats "affordable.")
- The em-dash (—) is a friend for asides and emphasis.

### Words & phrases we use

✅ Crew, hustle, careful, on time, no surprises, strong, padded, protected, transparent, real, simple, fair, lock it in, show up ready, the work, our own grandmother's dresser

### Words & phrases we avoid

❌ Premier, leverage, solutions, exceed expectations, white-glove (we're not), boutique, luxury, world-class, family-owned-and-operated (cliché), turnkey, holistic, synergy, seamless, journey

## Field vs Digital — the two modes

The brand operates in **two modes** with deliberately different color hierarchies. Same brand, different volume knob.

### Field mode (yard signs, flyers, vehicle wraps, banners, magnets)

- Orange: ~55%
- White/Cream: ~20%
- Navy: ~15%
- Squire Gold: ~7% (wordmark)
- Knight Red: ~3% (mascot)

**Why:** High visibility. Local recognition. People should see orange and think "Sir Box a Lot" by the third sign they pass.

### Digital mode (website, GBP, social, email)

- Cream/Paper backgrounds: ~50%
- Navy family: ~30%
- Stone/neutrals: ~10%
- Orange: ~7% (CTAs and accents only)
- Squire Gold: ~2% (wordmark/badges)
- Knight Red: ~1% (mascot illustration)

**Why:** Calmer reading experience. Trust signals via navy. Orange becomes the strategic punctuation mark — when it shows up, it means something (CTA, special offer, key claim).

## Typography

### Recommended pairing

| Role | Typeface | Where to use |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Display** (headlines) | **Archivo** (Bold/Black, 700–900) | H1–H2, signage type, business card name |
| **Body** (paragraphs, UI) | **Inter** (Regular/Medium, 400–500) | All body copy, web UI, captions |
| **Monospace** (specs) | **JetBrains Mono** | Rare — technical/detail contexts |

**Why Archivo + Inter:**

- Both free on Google Fonts and Adobe Fonts. Available everywhere.
- Archivo has sturdy, slightly humanistic letterforms — confident without being aggressive, friendly without being cute. Pairs well with the knight mascot.
- Inter is the most-readable body face for screens. Industry-standard for modern web.
- The combination reads as "modern, dependable, approachable" — the brand exactly.

### Web type hierarchy (desktop reference)

| Element | Typeface | Weight | Size |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| H1 | Archivo | 800 | 56–72px |
| H2 | Archivo | 700 | 36–48px |
| H3 | Archivo | 600 | 24–32px |
| Body | Inter | 400 | 16–18px |
| Small / captions | Inter | 400 | 13–14px |
| Buttons | Inter | 600 | 16px |

### Print recommendations

Same pairing. Body in print at 10–11pt Inter works well. Yard signs and large-format display: Archivo Black is the workhorse.

### Backup option for more personality

If Archivo + Inter feels too clean and you want more quirk: **Bricolage Grotesque (display) + Inter (body).** Bricolage has more character with a slightly playful geometric feel while staying professional. Free on Google Fonts.

## The mascot

### Character description

A friendly, confident cartoon knight in silver plate armor. Red plume on the helmet. Red cape. Brown leather belt with a gold buckle. Sword with a gold hilt. Carries a cardboard moving box. Slight smile, alert eyes. **Heroic but approachable** — not menacing, not goofy. A knight who'd help you move your couch.

### Master file (in progress)

The vector master is being commissioned via Fiverr / Upwork. See [🎨 Mascot Vector Redraw — Hiring Briefs](https://www.notion.so/35f0b55ac461815cb850d0fdc3d8fae5?pvs=21).

### The 5 canonical poses

1. **Hero pose** — standing, sword raised in one hand, box in the other. Matches current logo. Primary use everywhere.
2. **Friendly wave** — greeting pose, free hand waving. For intros, social profile graphics, "hi from us" content.
3. **Lifting action** — both hands hoisting a heavy box, slight knee bend. For service section graphics, "we do the heavy lifting" callouts.
4. **Tip-giving** — pointing up or finger raised in "pro tip" gesture. For moving-tips content, YouTube/social educational videos.
5. **Three-quarter walking** — mid-stride carrying a box, dynamic angle. For transitions in video, dynamic backgrounds.

### Usage rules

**Do:**

- ✅ Use the full-color version whenever possible — the mascot is the brand's personality, don't dilute him
- ✅ Maintain clear space around him equal to at least half his width on all sides
- ✅ Pair him with the wordmark, but they're never welded — both should breathe
- ✅ Place on Cream, White, Navy, or Orange backgrounds

**Don't:**

- ❌ Stretch, skew, or rotate beyond the canonical poses
- ❌ Recolor his cape, plume, or armor — those are fixed brand elements
- ❌ Remove the sword or box — they're part of his identity
- ❌ Place on busy photo backgrounds without a solid backplate
- ❌ Pair with other mascots, characters, or licensed IP
- ❌ Use AI to generate "new poses" — the 5 canonical poses are the entire library. More poses = recommissioning the illustrator.

### Size minimums

- Print: 0.5" wide minimum (any smaller, his face stops reading)
- Digital: 60px wide minimum
- Yard signs / banners: no upper limit

## Wordmark treatments

Two valid wordmark dress codes. Never mix both in a single composition.

### Yellow wordmark (with Navy outline)

- **Use for:** Outdoor signage, yard signs, vehicle wraps, banners, anywhere on an orange field
- **Why:** Yellow on the orange background punches harder than white would
- **Treatment:** "SIR BOX A LOT" in Squire Gold (`#FABE0A`) with Navy (`#0F1E32`) outline; "MOVERS" in white on an Orange (`#EB4100`) tab

### White wordmark (on Navy banner)

- **Use for:** Digital — website, GBP cover, FB profile, business cards, email signature, anywhere on light or photographic backgrounds
- **Why:** Cleaner. More refined. Reads better at small sizes.
- **Treatment:** "SIR BOX A LOT" in white on a Navy (`#0F1E32`) banner; "MOVERS" in white on an Orange tab

## Mascot voice & personality (for Higgsfield AI videos)

When the knight comes to life in animated tips videos, he speaks with these traits.

### Voice profile

- **Diction:** Slightly elevated but never archaic. Says "alright friends" not "hark." Says "let me show you" not "permit me to demonstrate."
- **Tone:** Confident craftsman. Like a senior tradesman who's seen every kind of move. Speaks with authority earned from doing the work, not from being a knight.
- **Pace:** Measured. Pauses for emphasis. Doesn't rush.
- **Posture:** Generous and direct. Doesn't condescend; doesn't beg for engagement.

### Mannerisms

- Owns the knight bit lightly. The character is medieval; the advice is modern.
- **Signature phrase (sparingly):** "By my honor" — used once per video as a callback, never twice.
- **Sign-off:** "Until the next move, friends." Or simply "Stay strong out there."
- **Catchphrase opener:** "Here's a tip from the road" — establishes practical voice from second one.

### Content rules

- **Every video must deliver one genuinely useful moving tip.** The character is the wrapper; the advice is the substance. Don't waste viewers' time with bit-only content.
- Length: 30–60 seconds for social / Shorts. Up to 2 min for YouTube proper.
- Always end with a soft CTA: "If you're moving in the Puget Sound, we'd be honored to help" or similar.

### What he doesn't do

- ❌ Use modern slang ("vibes," "no cap," "slay") — breaks character
- ❌ Use heavy medieval slang ("forsooth," "verily," "good morrow") — annoying after one viewing
- ❌ Speak about competitors (positive or negative)
- ❌ Make promises about specific times, dates, or pricing — those go in service copy, not the mascot's mouth
